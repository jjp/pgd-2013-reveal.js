A bit of self-defense

PhoneGap Self-defense for those of us who think like the web
A few places where I endured a large number of body blows, and thus a few areas you may wish to wear some extra padding as you make forays as a web developer into the app world with PhoneGap.

Where we* go wrong

* We = web developers

Core Web Concepts

Adherence to certain core web concepts can get us in trouble. Some things we have long held to be Absolutely The Way are different in the app world, subtlely or extremely.

We have certain patterns of thinking about the way the web works as a whole. I'm not talking about web standards, I'm talking about web concepts.

Nuance and Best Practices

Like it or not, we're on device in a native context.

As web developers, tend to fight platform specificity. It's in our nature. Or more than likely a specific device or devices. And because of that, we're beholden to certain patterns that will be new to us.

Process, Components and Tools

Suddenly, your workflow matters. A trusty text editor isn't enough anymore, there's tons of tedious detail you need to deal with

I'm somewhat irrationally proud of my "have text editor, will travel", environment-free way of developing and that no longer really cuts it. Suddenly I have to buckle down and deal with config-level minutiae that do not generally appeal to me as a web dev. But giving it short shrift can be fatal.

Complexity

And these problems compound as your app grows.

You'll be Wrangling Data

  • Caching
  • Synching
  • Offline mode

Wrangling Data: Caching

  • Default to keeping stuff locally
  • But: be mindful of localStorage or other quotas
  • Don't depend on localStorage
  • Lots of stuff? You probably want to use IndexedDB or something cool like that.

Wrangling Data: Synching

Your models are dirty, dirty, dirty

  • Synching local dirty data and doing a good job of it is a non-trivial problem
  • I'm not smart enough to tackle it myself, so I use things like backbone.dualStorage
  • Make it behave RESTfully just like remote sync to keep the chaos minimized
  • Nervous? You might consider readonly for local

Wrangling Data: Offline Mode

Finally a bit of good news: build your data wrangling carefully*, and offline mode can follow naturally.

If you want a bit more control:


// Hold that thought
document.addEventListener("offline", nobodyIsAnswering, false);
// Oh, we're all talking again!
document.addEventListener("online", hiThereHeresWhatChanged, false);

* Remember what I said about designing client storage patterns after RESTful remote sync?
